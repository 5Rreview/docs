# ✍️ Responding to Reviews

Best practices for companies responding to 5R★ Reviews on [5rrrrr.com](https://5rrrrr.com).

## Why Respond?

Response rate is a direct signal in your 5R★ Trust Score. Companies that respond to reviews — especially negative ones — build more trust than companies that stay silent.

## Best Practices

### For Negative Reviews

1. **Respond quickly** — response time is visible on your profile
2. **Acknowledge the issue** — don't dismiss or argue
3. **Offer a solution** — provide a concrete next step
4. **Follow through** — resolve the issue, then ask the reviewer to update their review
5. **Be specific** — reference the exact problem they described

**Good response:**
> "Thank you for sharing this. We identified the issue with your order and have processed a full refund. We've also updated our QA process to prevent this. Please don't hesitate to reach out if there's anything else."

**Avoid:**
> "We're sorry you had a bad experience. We always strive for excellence."

### For Positive Reviews

1. **Thank the reviewer** — acknowledge their time
2. **Be specific** — reference what they mentioned
3. **Keep it brief** — don't turn it into marketing

### For All Reviews

- Be professional and empathetic
- Never argue publicly
- Never offer compensation in exchange for review changes
- Don't use template responses for every review — personalize

## 🔄 The Resolution Loop

The most powerful feature for companies:

```
Negative review → Your response → Issue resolved → Reviewer updates → Score improves
```

When a reviewer updates their review after resolution, the platform shows both the original and updated versions — demonstrating that you take feedback seriously.

## 📈 Response Metrics

Your profile displays:
- **Response rate** — percentage of reviews with your response
- **Average response time** — how quickly you typically respond
- **Resolution rate** — percentage of reported issues you resolved

All three contribute positively to your 5R★ Trust Score.
